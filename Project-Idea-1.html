Overview

“Video games foster the mindset that allows creativity to grow.” – Nolan Bushnell. As technology progresses, conventional art and entertainment fail to keep up, paving the path for a new medium of storytelling: video games. Since my ideal job is to work in the gaming industry, for this Project Idea, I am proposing a concept for a third-person action-adventure video game set in the historical periods of Vietnam with the use of Unreal Engine, recounting stories of our triumphs and accomplishments, called An Nam. This project will be the product of my passion for the field of Information Technology, and my affection for my country’s cultures and customs.

Motivation

02

The Video Game Industry (or Gaming Industry) is indisputably one of the largest and fastest growing, even exceeding those of the Film Industry and Digital TV & Video Industry. Despite such significant economic potential, the industry in Vietnam seems to be stagnating and lagging behind, concentrating on importing foreign games and localising them instead of creating meaningful and impactful culture-rich games. Furthermore, as video games play a fundamental role in the development of certain technology advancements (for instance the graphics cards), the lack of large-scale gaming projects in Vietnam might inhibit its people from exploring this sector of Information Technology. Understanding and endeavouring to rectify these issues, I want to create An Nam, a project that could revitalise the game development spirit in the country while leaving a legacy spreading Vietnamese history and traditions to foreign lands.

Great video games have good stories, but masterpieces require both phenomenal storytelling and plot. An Nam is set in the historical eras of Vietnam, and you will play as multiple different historical figures as the game progresses. Currently, I plan to have 3 main protagonists:
- Ngo Quyen: a warlord who ended the thousand-year domination of Chinese dynasties and founded the kingdom of An Nam. 
- Ly Thuong Kiet: a great general of the Ly Dynasty, the author of the first declaration of independence of Vietnam 
- Tran Hung Dao: a royal prince and great general who repelled two major Mongol invasions. 
Generally, the plot structure for all protagonists will be relatively similar, spanning their birth, adolescent years, major accomplishments and ultimately, their death. With this structure, the players will be able to experience the full life of the characters, growing fond of them gradually, allowing them to immerse in the historical setting more convincingly. All characters will be voice acted in Vietnamese and subtitled in different languages, ensuring accessibility for international audiences. The plot and quest designs will also be written with consultation from historians to emphasise the customs and characteristics of the Vietnamese people, fulfilling one of the main objectives of the game: spreading the Vietnamese heritage to the world.

The Narrative: Great video games have good stories, but masterpieces require both phenomenal storytelling and plot. An Nam is set in the historical eras of Vietnam, and you will play as multiple different historical figures as the game progresses. Currently, I plan to have 3 main protagonists:
- Ngo Quyen: a warlord who ended the thousand-year domination of Chinese dynasties and founded the kingdom of An Nam. 
- Ly Thuong Kiet: a great general of the Ly Dynasty, the author of the first declaration of independence of Vietnam 
- Tran Hung Dao: a royal prince and great general who repelled two major Mongol invasions. 
Generally, the plot structure for all protagonists will be relatively similar, spanning their birth, adolescent years, major accomplishments and ultimately, their death. With this structure, the players will be able to experience the full life of the characters, growing fond of them gradually, allowing them to immerse in the historical setting more convincingly. All characters will be voice acted in Vietnamese and subtitled in different languages, ensuring accessibility for international audiences. The plot and quest designs will also be written with consultation from historians to emphasise the customs and characteristics of the Vietnamese people, fulfilling one of the main objectives of the game: spreading the Vietnamese heritage to the world. 

The Gameplay: Gameplay mechanics are arguably the most challenging aspect of game development. In a historically accurate game like An Nam, the primary combat system will be melee combat, using a variety of weapons including spears, staffs, short swords, "daos” and shields. A limited range combat system using bows and crossbows will also be implemented. A combat skill-tree will not be used in order to maintain historical accuracy, instead players will unlock new abilities as the story progresses. Similarly, to remain faithful to factual events, dialogues will be linear, not branching. While the game is level-based and not intended to be open-world, each level should be large enough to encompass the look and feel of the Monarchical period of Vietnam. Within the confine of these levels, certain traversal mechanics will be in place, such as carriages and horseback riding, along with exploration opportunities. 

The Technical Aspects: The success of a video game depends on how immersive it is, and the same principle will be applied to An Nam. In order to maintain the players’ suspension of disbelief, the game needs to be designed intricately and polished inside out. The most important aspect when it comes to immersion  is undoubtedly the graphics. Since a detailed game with Vietnamese characters is rather unprecedented, 3D character models will need to be designed from scratch using Blender. Environment design and textures will be taken from archaeological findings, historical documents and still-standing monuments. All of these details will be incorporated into Unreal Engine to be assembled for level construction. On the programming department, the game will have to be coded with care, ensuring a minimal number of bugs and physics irregularity. Certain subsystems offered by Unreal Engine like Physics and NPC AI will be utilised to deliver a more entrancing experience. Last but not least, an often overlooked but equally important aspect is sound design. The auditory experience in a video game could be the “make or break” factor, and thus for An Nam, the sound engineering will have to be carefully catered to. Era-appropriate soundtracks will be recorded, and as previously stated, voice acting will be done with Vietnamese voice artists, giving the game another level of ambience.
